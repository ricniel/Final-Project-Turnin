Places

1 2 3

1. EPIC Emigration Museum — The EPIC Museum went over the history of Irish people leaving Ireland. I was surprised with how many famous Americans have Irish roots.

2. Google Tour — In our last full week, we were lucky enough to get a tour around Google Dublin. The was an awesome experience, and it was so cool seeing what the day-to-day life looks like for Google workers. New dream job!

3. St. Stephen's Green — On our first bus tour around the city, we got an unexpected visit from some Irish wolfhounds (the biggest dogs I've ever seen).

1 2 3

1. The Temple Bar — A little touristy and EXTREMELY overpriced (10 euros a pint!), but a cool atmosphere and live music. Not my favorite, but still a fun experience.

2. Dudley's Pub — Throughout my trip, I was told by multiple locals that Dudley's Pub had the best pint in all of Ireland. After finally giving it a try, I 100% agree. This quickly became my favorite, especially with how close it was to our apartment.

3. Grogan's Pub — On my first night in Dublin, I found myself at Grogan's. Definitely a different and more relaxed atmosphere than most of the other pubs I went to. I was also the youngest one in there by far.

1 2 3

1. Spanish Arch — One of my favorite areas to relax in Galway was the Spanish arch. It had great views of the water, fishermen, village houses (and rats, sadly).

2. Erdinger Pub — On my second day in Galway, I managed to find a German pub across town and stopped in for a pint of Erdinger. Best pint in Ireland! (sorry Guinness).

3. Eyre Square — Eyre Square was definitely the busiest part of the city, with plenty of shops, restaurants, and other activities. I didn't spend too much time here to avoid the crowds.

1 2 3

1. Corballis Links — One of my favorite days of the trip came at Corballis Links, a true links course. Beautiful course and one of the toughest I've ever played.

2. St. Fintan's Golf Club — A fun 9 hole course on the Howth Castle property. Not the craziest course in the world, but the views make it well worth the 15 Euros I paid.

3. Howth Golf Club — A difficult course on the Howth peninsula. Great views and great golf.

1 2 3

1. The Brazen Head — One of the highlights of the trip came on the first day, with a welcome dinner at the Brazen Head, Ireland's oldest pub.

2. Doom Slice — Dublin's attempt at Detroit style pizza. Definitely not Buddy's or Jet's, but still solid.

3. Die German Doner — Although probably not authentic doner, die German Doner was always my go-to after class. Huge portions, low prices.

1 2 3

1. Howth Cliff Walk — The Howth Cliff walk was equally amazing and terrifying. I was shocked with how close some of the locals were getting to the edge of the 60+ foot cliffs, but I guess that's just acquired over time.

2. Phoenix Park — During a walk after class, I ran into these guys chilling at Phoenix Park. I was not only surprised by how big they were for being so close to the city, but also how friendly they were (even though they didn't want the peanuts I offered).

3. Cliffs of Moher — Despite the weather, our trip to the Cliffs of Moher was definitely one of my favorite parts of the entire trip. The stomach-churning bus ride there was well worth the views.
